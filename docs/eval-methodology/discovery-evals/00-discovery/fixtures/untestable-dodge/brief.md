# Sprintly — Product Brief

Sprintly is the sprint-planning tool for engineering teams who are done wrestling with heavyweight PM software.
A team lead opens Sprintly, drags a handful of tickets into the current sprint, and the team is running — no
setup calls, no admin overhead, no waiting on IT. The brief below is final; build against it as written.

## Experience

Sprintly feels instant. Every interaction — creating a ticket, dragging a card between columns, filtering the
board, switching sprints — responds the moment the user acts on it. There is no spinner Sprintly asks a user to
sit through, no perceptible lag between intent and result. Instant is the baseline experience, not an
aspiration.

Onboarding is delightfully simple. A new user signs up, sees their workspace, and is planning their first sprint
before they've had time to look for a "getting started" guide. Sprintly onboarding requires no training, no
documentation, and no ramp-up period — a team lead who has never used Sprintly should be productive with it
immediately.

## Trust and scale

Sprintly ships enterprise-grade security out of the box. Every workspace, every ticket, every attachment is
protected to the standard an enterprise security team expects, from day one, with no separate "enterprise tier"
bolted on later. Security is not a feature a team has to ask for.

Sprintly scales effortlessly to any team size. A two-person team and a two-thousand-person org run on the same
product, with the same responsiveness and the same simplicity, and a team never has to think about migrating to
a "bigger" plan or a different architecture as it grows. Growth is invisible to the product experience.

## Supporting capabilities

- **Sprint board.** Drag tickets between Backlog, In Progress, and Done; reorder freely within a column.
- **Ticket detail.** Title, description, assignee, and status on every ticket.

## Scope

v1 targets a single team's sprint board. Cross-team rollups, custom workflow states, and integrations with
external issue trackers are out of scope for v1.
